# Retail & Recreation Post COVID Era

## Intro and Overview
**Research Question**

How did the COVID-19 pandemic affect retail and recreation transport across U.S. states and counties, and how does Californias disruption and recovery compare?

**Reasoning**
- What is retail and recreation?
    -  Mobility trends for places like restaurants, cafes, shopping centers, theme parks, museums, libraries, and movie theaters.
- Why focus on that?
    - It isnt a necessary mobility category like Residential, Workplaces, and Grocery & pharmacy
    - A return to baseline would reflect a cultural return to "normal", and any % change from baseline may reflect cultural changes and recovery from the pandemic

**Overview of the dataset** 
- https://www.gstatic.com/covid19/mobility/2022-10-15_US_Mobility_Report_en.pdf

- Data collection:
    - The mobility data is collected from users’ mobile devices via Google’s Location History feature. 
    - Only users who have opted in to Location History (which is off by default) are included in the dataset
    - If there aren’t enough devices contributing data in a category, the data is not reported for a given day or location category

- What's mobility change?
    - “mobility” refers to these changes in how often people visit or how long they stay at different types of locations 
    - mobility change indicates how much more or less people are moving around relative to pre-COVID levels (the baseline)
    - mobility change data is from march 2020

- What is the baseline and what does it represent?
    - The baseline is the median value, for the corresponding day of the week, during the 5-week period Jan 3–Feb 6, 2020.

## Conclusion & Discussion 
- California experienced both deeper initial disruption and slower, less complete recovery compared to most other states
- Urban coastal counties, particularly the Bay Area, drove much of California's poor performance
- Longer-lasting restrictions or behavioral changes in urban California
- Tourism-dependent economies
